Background

Kenyan-born US citizen and US Army veteran. Based in Baltimore, Maryland. Twelve months in Nairobi (April 2025–2026) embedded in Kenya's governance and civic technology environment during a period of significant institutional disruption — Gen Z-led accountability protests, a contested Tanzanian election, and a consequential year in US domestic policy, all read simultaneously from a position inside neither country.

The civic tech portfolio is the practical output of that vantage point: AI infrastructure that reduces information asymmetry for East African communities and their global diaspora. Everything is open-source, bilingual where it matters, and built for the constraints of the people it serves.

Available for collaboration, consulting engagements, and full-time roles in AI engineering, data infrastructure, or systems analysis — particularly where East African context, institutional knowledge, or cleared/security-sensitive environments are relevant.
